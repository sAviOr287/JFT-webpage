City guide

Ten Oxford beer gardens worth planning a summer ride for.

A hand-picked list from years spent studying in the city—balancing tranquil meadows, historic taverns, and the perfect pint after a day of research. Pack a picnic blanket, take a bike, and follow the map.

Oxford August 9, 2018

Plot your route

Start near Port Meadow, trace the Isis, and meander back through Jericho and Cowley. Tap on a pin to load the corresponding directions.

The gardens

The Plough — Port Meadow

A meadow-side pause beloved by walkers and rowers. Expect a playground for families, seasonal menus that occasionally overachieve, and sunsets that feel miles away from city bustle.

The Green, Upper Wolvercote, OX2 8BD — theploughoxford.co.uk

The Perch — Binsey

One of Oxford’s oldest pubs, with a thatched roof, riverside walks, and a legendary summer garden. Continue north for the Trout Inn and Jacobs Inn if you’re extending the ride.

Binsey Lane, OX2 0NG — the-perch.co.uk

The Victoria — Jericho

A whisky-lover’s haven with a pie menu worth the detour. Grab a seat on the front terrace and stay for the taxidermy conversation starters.

90 Walton Street, OX2 6EB — victorianpub.co.uk

The Turf Tavern — City centre

Hidden down narrow alleys, this historic courtyard has hosted prime ministers and students racing the yard of ale. It’s peak Oxford—especially after exams.

4-5 Bath Place, OX1 3SU — greeneking-pubs.co.uk

The Angel & Greyhound — St Clement’s

Straddling the bustle of St Clement’s Street and the calm of its meadow, this spot mixes pool tables, board games, and a breezy patio.

30 St Clement’s Street, OX4 1AB — angelandgreyhound.co.uk

The Port Mahon — St Clement’s

A cosy alternative just down the road, famous for its Sunday quiz and back-garden leather sofas—perfect for a slow evening with friends.

82 St Clement’s Street, OX4 1AW

The Star — Rectory Road

East Oxford spirit distilled into a backyard with real grass, ample seating, and enough character to keep you until the fairy lights turn on.

21 Rectory Road, OX4 1BU

The Cowley Retreat — Cowley Road

Student HQ for lively evenings, strung with fairy lights and backed by an upbeat playlist. Perfect for gathering a crowd before heading deeper into Cowley.

172 Cowley Road, OX4 1UE — thecowleyretreat.com

The Rusty Bicycle — Magdalen Road

Brunch-friendly, community-driven, and part of the Dodo Pub Co family. Try the mighty veggie breakfast or settle in for an evening with the neighbourhood regulars.

28 Magdalen Road, OX4 1RB — therustybicycle.com

The Isis Farmhouse — Iffley Lock

Follow the towpath south for riverside jazz on Sunday afternoons, roaring fireplaces in winter, and one of the city’s most generous lawns.

Haystacks Corner, Iffley Lock, OX4 4EL — theisisfarmhouse.co.uk

Honourable mentions

Tapas & cocktails

Kazbar — Cowley Road

Not a beer garden, but the sun-drenched veranda, tapas, and multilingual banter from the team deliver a vibrant Andalusian detour in the heart of Oxford.

25-27 Cowley Road, OX4 1HP — kazbar.co.uk

Tea time

Vaults & Garden — Radcliffe Square

Sit beneath the University Church spire, sip a lapsang souchong, and admire the Radcliffe Camera. Cream tea meets architectural wonder.

1 Radcliffe Square, OX1 4AH — thevaultsandgarden.com

Make it your own

Oxford’s hospitality scene evolves—if you discover a new favourite nook, I’d love to hear about it. The guide is updated whenever I’m back in town.

Pairings & playlists

Queue up some mellow jazz, bring friends, and alternate between the meadow stretches and Cowley’s vibrant nights. Responsible revelry encouraged.
